America, Inc.

In 1908, Henry Ford began producing and selling the Ford Model T, decimating the formerly robust buggy and whip industry. More recently, Amazon has displaced booksellers and companies in the technology space have dominated the economy in both in terms of overall market cap and growth. See how the American economy has evolved over the years, both in terms of what the most successful companies do and how big they are.

Wal-Mart Stores

From its humble origins in Bentonville, AR, Wal-Mart Stores has grown to be the largest retailer in the world. Wal-Mart pioneered the model of superstore retailing we know today, and it has paid off. Wal-Mart entered the top ten of the domestic Fortune 500 in 1995 and has since skyrocketed to the top of the Fortune Global 500, with 11,000 stores in 27 countries. To this day, though, Wal-Mart is based in Arkansas and managed by the Walton family.

Rockefeller's Legacy

In 1880, Standard Oil Company held 90% of US refining capacity, a monopoly if there ever was one. In 1911, the Supreme Court forced the breakup of the company into 34 smaller companies, citing the Sherman Antitrust Act. These smaller companies have thrived in the American market and continue to play an important role in our oil economy today. (Featured are Exxon, Mobil, Chevron, Pennzoil, Atlantic Richfield, and Amoco).
